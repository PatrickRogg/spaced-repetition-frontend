Active Recall

Spaced Repetition

As the name suggests the repetition of subjects is spaced out over a period of time. A topic is reviewed at particular intervals to forgetting it. The concept of learning something once and losing memory about it over time is also called the Forgetting Curve.

The Forgetting Curve
Ebbinghaus' forgetting curve and review cycle.

With Spaced Repetition we interrupt the Forgetting Curve by repeating of what we have learned. That way we encode information into our long-term memory.
