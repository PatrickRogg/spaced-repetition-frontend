Tutorial

This Tutorial will show you how to use our Spaced Repetition system effectively. You will learn how to create Flashcard Decks, Flashcard and how you can repeat them. The first step is to Sign Up to create an account.

The best way to follow this tutorial is to either put the Tutorial page on a second monitor. Alternatively put two browser windows side by side. If you are on mobile open a second tab and switch between them.

Step 1

If you aren't already on the dashboard. Click Dashboard in the Navigation bar.

The first step is to create a Flashcard Deck. A Flashcard Deck or Deck is used to group your Flashcards into different subjects (Math, Computer Science, Spanish etc.) or categories. Click Add Flashcard Deck, enter a name in the popup and click Save Flashcard Deck.

Step 2

Click on the Flashcard Deck under My Decks that you just created. On this page you can see all your Flashcards. Each Deck can have any number of Topics. A Topic can be used to further divide your Flashcards. Rename the Topic to your liking. Afterwards click New Flashcard.

Step 3

In this popup you can now create your first Flashcard. Enter a Question and Answer of your choice. The text in the Answer field can be formatted and you can also paste in images. If you imagine a Flashcard the Question will be the front side and the Answer the backside of your Flashcard.

When you filled in Question and Answer. Click Add Flashcard.

Step 4

Now you can see the created Flashcard in your Topic. The Flashcard also has a Level and Last Wrong Answer property. The Level tells you how often you answered the Card correctly in a row and the Last Wrong Answer is the date of your last wrong answer for this Flashcard.

To see the details click on the name of the Flashcard you created.

Step 5

Here you can see and change the Question and Answer you entered before. Additionally, you can edit the Level and the Last Wrong Answer. To continue with the Tutorial change the Last Wrong Answer date to yesterday. This step is only necessary for Tutorial purposes and normally you don't have to do this. Click the Save button and go back to the Dashboard.

Step 6
Now you see that you have 1 Flashcard that is TODO. These are the Flashcards that you have to repeat today. You can either use the Start Repetition button at the top. Or if you only want to repeat Cards from a specific deck. Click the play button to the right of the Flashcard Deck.
Step 7

Now you are in your Repetition Session. You can see how many remaining cards you have left in the top left corner. If you want to quit the Repetition Session use the cross in the top right corner.

Below that you can see the Question of the current Flashcard. Read the Question and answer it by saying it out loud. When you are done click Show Answer.

Step 8

This shows you the answer of your Flashcard. Read it and check if your answer was correct. If it was correct click Answer Correct otherwise Answer wrong. Afterwards your Flashcard are automatically scheduled for its next repetition. The only thing you have to do is to come back and keep repeating your daily open cards.

Additionally, this view allows you to edit your Flashcard by clicking the pencil icon if you to want make any changes.

Step 9

The stats page gives you more detail about your Reptition Session. You can view all cards that you completed and get a better understanding about how well you did. If you made a mistake during your Repetition Session you can always change the cards Level or Last Wrong Answer to fix it.
